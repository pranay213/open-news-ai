**How to Get Started Quickly**

1. **Validate Locally** – Talk to a few potential customers (e.g., a local restaurant for AR overlays, a community centre for Pop‑Up Skill Labs) before you invest heavily.
2. **Leverage Existing Platforms** – Use affordable hardware (Arduino, Raspberry Pi, low‑cost air sensors) and SaaS tools (Stripe for payments, Firebase for data storage) to keep startup costs low.
3. **Pilot & Iterate** – Run a 2‑week pilot, collect feedback, refine the service, then roll out a paid version.
4. **Build Trust Early** – For data‑oriented ideas (air quality, digital legacy), emphasize privacy, clear consent, and transparent pricing.
5. **Create Simple Marketing Assets** – Short demo videos, before‑after case studies, and a concise one‑page pitch deck can be enough to land the first client.

These five concepts are deliberately off‑the‑beaten‑path, scalable, and can be launched with modest seed capital while still offering a clear path to revenue. Good luck!
